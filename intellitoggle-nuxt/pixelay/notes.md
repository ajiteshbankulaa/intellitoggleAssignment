## Pixelay vs Current Implementation Notes

- Overall, the current implementation matches the Pixelay Figma design fairly closely in structure and layout. The main sections, hierarchy, and general visual composition align well with the intended design.
- Typography and major UI elements translate well from the design to the actual implementation. Headings, call-to-action buttons, and major content blocks largely reflect the proportions and emphasis shown in Pixelay.


### Differences / Areas that Deviate

- Some spacing and margin behavior differs slightly from the Figma version. This is most noticeable around the hero spacing and the transition between the header and the hero content on mobile.
- A few sections feel slightly tighter or looser than the original Pixelay layout, likely due to Tailwind spacing approximations rather than exact pixel matches.
- Minor differences in font rendering and line wrapping appear due to browser typography behavior compared to Figma.

### Overall Assessment

- The implementation is close to the Pixelay design overall, especially in terms of structure, visual hierarchy, and core layout.
- Most remaining differences are **fine-tuning issues (spacing, breakpoints, and exact alignment)** rather than structural mismatches.
